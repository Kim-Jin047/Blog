Attentional vigilance of food information in disordered eating behaviors

Nanjing University of Chinese Medicine, Nanjing, China

1ST AUTHOR

2023

  • Research Content: This study examined attentional patterns toward food-related stimuli in individuals with disordered eating behaviors using the Exogenous Cueing Task (ECT). Results showed that participants with disordered eating exhibited significantly greater attentional engagement with food stimuli, suggesting heightened vigilance toward food cues as a distinct attentional bias in this population.
  • Responsibilities: Conducted experimental design and implementation, participant recruitment, and data collection based on a visual attention paradigm. Performed statistical analysis using SPSS with a focus on variance components of attentional engagement, and contributed to all stages of manuscript preparation, including hypothesis formulation, result interpretation, and academic writing.

Relationship power attenuated the effects of gratitude on perceived partner responsiveness and satisfaction in romantic relationships

East China Normal University, Shanghai, China

1ST AUTHOR

2024

  • Research Content: This study explored how relationship power moderates the associations between gratitude, perceived partner responsiveness, and relationship satisfaction. Based on the relationship maintenance model and social distance theory, an online survey was conducted among 825 participants to examine the mediating role of perceived responsiveness and the moderating role of power asymmetry in romantic relationships.
  • Responsibilities: Designed the research framework and survey instruments, organized large-scale online data collection, and performed mediation and moderation analyses using SPSS. Responsible for interpreting findings considering contemporary social and relationship psychology theories and composing the manuscript for submission.

Positive resonance: Successful interpersonal emotion regulation facilitated relationship closeness by enhanced inter-brain synchronization

East China Normal University, Shanghai, China

3RD AUTHOR

2025
